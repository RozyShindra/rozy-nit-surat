Privacy-Preservation in Quantum Natural Language Processing for Secure Applications

This research constitutes my M.Tech thesis, conducted in collaboration with Dr. Mukesh A. Zaveri (SVNIT, Surat) and Dr. Rutal Mahajan (Utica University, USA)

This thesis presents a lightweight privacy-preserving NLP pipeline that encrypts Personally Identifiable Information (PII) while keeping the text readable and usable for downstream tasks. A fine-tuned keyphrase extractor is used to identify sensitive information, which is then securely transformed. We leverage Quantum Natural Language Processing (QNLP) for semantic representation and processing, enabling richer context understanding and faster computation. The approach has been benchmarked against classical machine learning models, standard privacy techniques, and parallel processing frameworks. Results show that QNLP can offer competitive accuracy with enhanced privacy and computational efficiency.
